Building in Public:
Why I'm Documenting This

June 2026 Dev journey

I'm a pharmacist. I manage an infectious disease infusion clinic. I have no formal background in software development, and until recently, no real reason to think I'd be building anything.

Then I started building Dosecraft — a simulation platform for infusion patients — and realized I needed to actually learn how any of this works. This site is part of that process.

Why write about it?

Mostly accountability. It's easy to tinker indefinitely without shipping anything. Writing forces a kind of clarity that just building doesn't — you have to actually articulate what you did, why it didn't work, and what you tried next.

There's also something worth documenting about learning software development as a clinician. Most dev content assumes you want to be a developer. I don't, exactly. I want to build a specific thing, and I'm learning what I need to learn to do that.

The goal isn't to become a programmer. The goal is to build something that helps patients understand their treatment.

If that distinction resonates with you — if you're a domain expert teaching yourself to build — maybe some of this is useful.

What this site is for

This is a place to document Dosecraft, share experiments, and be honest about what I don't know. It's not a polished portfolio. It's closer to a public notebook.
